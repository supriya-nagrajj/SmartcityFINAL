About Mumbai

Mumbai, often called the "City of Dreams," is the financial capital of India and home to Bollywood. Known for its iconic landmarks like the Gateway of India, Marine Drive, and Elephanta Caves, the city offers a blend of rich history, vibrant culture, and modernity. With its bustling local trains, street food, and diverse population, Mumbai truly reflects the spirit of India.

World Heritage Sites in Mumbai

Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (Victoria Terminus)

CST is the main train station in Mumbai, and the main station in western India as well. Opening in 1887, it took ten years to build! Built to commemorate Queen Victoria's Golden Jubilee, the station was known as Victoria Terminus until 1999. It's a supreme example of Victorian Gothic architecture, and is partly based on St Pancras railway station in London. The attention to detail here is just marvellous, with beautiful domes, arches, statues and turrets, both inside and out. There's also a magnificent entry gate loaded with symbolism, crowned by a British lion and an Indian tiger.

Elephanta Caves

Elephanta Caves are located on an island in the middle of Mumbai harbour, just a short boat ride from the iconic Gateway of India. This site covers five man-made caves dug directly into the side of the mountain. The caves are dedicated to the worship of Shiva, one of the main Hindu gods, and they're a spectacular sight to behold. The main cave here is immense - roughly square shaped, 40 metres on each side, and nearly 10 metres tall. The walls are all covered in carvings depicting the stories of Shiva, done with exquisite detail. But the real highlight here is the Trimurti, a six metre tall relief sculpture of a three-headed Shiva.

Victorian Gothic and Art Deco Ensembles of Mumbai

Located in the Fort area of downtown Mumbai, this collection of 94 beautiful buildings dates from the 19th and early 20th centuries. They’re a legacy of the British colonial period. Rich with cash from exports, the local government built enormous grand buildings to make Bombay world-renowned. Highlights include the Bombay High Court, the University of Mumbai, and the City Civil and Sessions building. The CST railway station is also a part of this grand architectural heritage.

Travel Tips for Mumbai

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Mumbai is from October to March when the weather is pleasant.

Local Transport

Use local trains, buses, and auto-rickshaws for economical travel around the city.

Street Food

Don’t miss the iconic Vada Pav, Pav Bhaji, and Bhel Puri from Mumbai’s street vendors.
